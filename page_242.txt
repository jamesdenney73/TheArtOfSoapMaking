by adding from 2 to 23 gallons of pure water. After
stirring a few minutes the homogeneity of the soap is re-
established and the combination perfected. The ‘heat is
then withdrawn, and the soap transferred to the frames as
usual. After five or six days the soap is firm enough to
cut. By the above process the soap is very white, does not
contain any excess of alkali or oil, and may be employed
for toilet uses. From the quantities given from 396 to
400 Ibs. of soap are obtained, according to the quantity
