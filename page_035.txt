The Ley Tanks, containing caustic alkali of various
ascertained strengths, are sometimes placed at one end of
the series of soap-pans, and at a suitable height above
them, so that the leys may be conveniently run off by iron
shoots into each pan, by turning the tap connected to
either tank. These tanks are commonly made of wrought
iron plates riveted together.
