Since toilet soaps are required to be somewhat firmer
and harder than ordinary household soaps, a certain
amount of evaporation of their combined water must be
allowed to take place during the re-melting; but this must
not be carried too far, otherwise the soap will be liable to
crack during the subsequent pressing or stamping opera-
tions. Again, it will be necessary to evaporate a portion
of the combined water to allow for the addition of the
essential oils or perfumes which are to be blended with it.

When dry colouring matters, as vermilion, yellow-
ochre, red-lead, and various metallic oxides have to be
mixed with the melted soap, care must be taken not to
allow the paste to become too stiff, otherwise, when these
are incorporated with the mass, it may become unmanage-
able.
