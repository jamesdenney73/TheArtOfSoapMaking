According to Richardson and Watt it is better to
saponify the resin and tallow separately, and to mix the
two soaps in the pan, and then to boil until a perfect
union takes place. Salt is then added, and the soap
finished in the ordinary way.

The usual proportions of palm-oil and resin are 3}
parts of the former to 1 part of the latter.
