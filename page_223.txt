At the same time that the soap solution is decomposed,
the fat and resin acids rise to the surface, and these are
collected on a weighed filter, washed with hot water, dried
in vacuo, and again weighed. The weight expresses the
joint amount of fat and resin acids in the soap. Cold
alcohol will dissolve out all the fat acid, together with a
small proportion of the resin from the filter, and the
filter dried 7” vacuo and weighed as before, gives approxi-
mately the amount of resin in the soap.

To determine whether the base of the soap is soda or
potash, the solution of the sulphates filtered from the fat
acids is conventrated and treated with tartaric acid and
dichloride of platinum in the usual way. The filter
containing the matter insoluble in alcohol is dried and
weighed, after being thoroughly washed in alcohol. In
genuine soap this insoluble matter is of very small amount,
not exceeding 1 per cent. for mottled and even less for
yellow soap.
