Belgian Soap.—In Belgium, a half-hard soap is largely
produced for the use of cloth manufacturers, and is
employed in scouring woollen textile fabrics. This soap
contains an excess of alkali (potash), an essential feature in
soaps employed for this purpose. The caustic ley is used at
three different degrees of strength, namely, 18°, 20°, and °
80° Baumé, and these represent the first, second, and third
leys used in the preparation of this soap. The fatty
