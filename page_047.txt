China clay, or Kaolin, is sometimes used as an adulterant
in the manufacture of some of the cheaper soaps.

Sulphate of Soda, or Glauber’s Salt, is also extensively
used in combination with soaps of the cheaper kind, the
mixture producing a soap of considerable hardness, while
reducing its percentage of fatty material.
